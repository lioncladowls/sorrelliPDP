# PDP Best Practices — Conversion Optimization Reference

Research compiled from Baymard Institute, NNGroup, CXL, ConvertCart, Foursixty, Commerce-UI, and industry sources (2024–2026). Focused on fashion jewelry, women who self-shop, and aspirational accessible luxury positioning.

---

## 1. Image Gallery

The gallery is the single most important conversion element on a jewelry PDP. 67% of online shoppers say image quality is the #1 factor in purchase decisions. High-quality visuals convert at rates up to 94% higher than minimal imagery.

### What to include (in order)
1. **Hero product shot** — clean, white background, high detail, the "standard" ecommerce image
2. **On-body / lifestyle shot** — jewelry on a model (ear, hand, neck). Lifestyle photos convert 3x better than plain product shots. For jewelry specifically, buyers need body context for scale reference.
3. **Alternate angle** — show the piece from a different perspective (side, back, clasp detail)
4. **Detail / macro shot** — crystal facets, metalwork, setting construction. This replaces the in-store "pick it up and look closely" moment.
5. **Lifestyle / styled shot** — the piece in an aspirational context (vanity, curated flat lay). This sells the feeling, not just the product.
6. **Video or GIF** — motion catches attention and extends engagement time. Even a simple rotating view adds perceived quality.
7. **UGC / customer photo** — real customers wearing the piece. 92% of shoppers rely on peer feedback when purchasing.

### Gallery UX
- For galleries with 5+ images, use a horizontal thumbnail strip with navigation arrows and one highlighted image at a time
- Enable zoom on desktop (pinch-zoom on mobile)
- Image should dominate the viewport — 60-90% of above-fold space on desktop
- Swipeable on mobile with clear indicator dots
- Consider full-width immersive galleries for premium feel (On, ARK/8, Gucci pattern)

### Sorrelli-specific opportunity
82% of top-performing jewelry brands use multiple photography styles per listing. We have AI-generated lifestyle, studio, and on-body images from the Image Gen project — use the full range.

## 2. Above-the-Fold Hierarchy

60%+ of traffic lands directly on PDPs. What's visible without scrolling determines whether shoppers engage or bounce.

### Must be above the fold
1. **Product image** (dominant)
2. **Product name**
3. **Price** (with BNPL breakdown if applicable)
4. **Star rating / review count** — visible near the price. Builds instant trust.
5. **Color/finish swatches** — interactive, showing all available options
6. **Add to Bag CTA** — prominent, impossible to miss
7. **Availability / shipping signal** — "In Stock — Ships in 1-3 days"

### Should NOT be above the fold
- Brand story content (save for scrolling discovery)
- FAQ sections
- Cross-sell recommendations
- Detailed product specs (put in expandable tabs)

### Key principle
Users DO scroll — but only if what's above the fold is compelling enough. Above-fold is for conversion mechanics; below-fold is for storytelling, trust-building, and cross-selling.

## 3. Mobile-First Design

73% of ecommerce traffic and 58% of purchases happen on mobile. Over 70% of jewelry ecommerce traffic specifically is mobile.

### Critical mobile patterns
- **Sticky Add to Cart bar** — keeps the CTA visible as users scroll. Must include product name, price, and the button. Should feel helpful, not intrusive.
- **Tap targets minimum 44x44px** — especially for swatches, buttons, and gallery navigation
- **Vertical layout** — single column, image on top, info below
- **Thumb-zone awareness** — primary actions (CTA, swatches) should be in the comfortable thumb reach area (center-bottom of screen)
- **Progressive disclosure** — use expandable sections for details, care instructions, shipping info. Don't dump everything in a scroll wall.
- **Fast load** — as load time goes from 1s to 3s, bounce probability increases 32%

### Mobile gallery
- Full-width swipeable images
- Dot indicators for position
- Pinch-to-zoom
- Video should autoplay muted on scroll-into-view

## 4. CTA (Add to Cart) Optimization

### Button design
- High contrast against the page background
- Action-oriented text: "Add to Bag" outperforms "Add to Cart" for fashion/luxury
- Include the price in the button text (e.g., "Add to Bag — $80") for price reinforcement
- Full-width on mobile
- Consider a secondary CTA like "Consult an Expert" or "Find in Store" for higher-consideration pieces

### Placement
- Primary CTA above the fold
- Sticky CTA on mobile (activates after first scroll past the original button)
- Repeated CTA further down the page for long-scroll PDPs
- Near trust signals (shipping info, return policy, payment icons)

### Urgency signals (use sparingly and authentically)
- "In Stock — Ships Today" with dynamic date calculation
- Low stock indicators when genuinely limited
- Scarcity signals lift conversion 8-32% in A/B tests — but only when legitimate

## 5. Price Presentation for Accessible Luxury

Sorrelli sits in a specific pricing psychology zone: not cheap enough for impulse, not expensive enough to require consultative selling. The $40-150 range needs particular treatment.

### Principles
- **Use clean, rounded pricing** — premium brands avoid charm pricing ($79.99). $80.00 reads as more confident and luxurious than $79.99.
- **Show BNPL prominently** — "4 interest-free payments of $20.00" makes $80 feel very accessible. BNPL is repeatedly proven effective for jewelry in the $40-150 range.
- **Don't lead with discounts** — for aspirational brands, leading with "30% OFF" cheapens perception. Show the value first, discount second.
- **Compare-at pricing when applicable** — showing a crossed-out original price provides an anchor. The anchor must be believable.
- **Frame the price in context** — "Less than your daily coffee habit for a piece that lasts a lifetime" type messaging reinforces value.

### What NOT to do
- Don't use $.99 pricing on fashion jewelry — it signals discount/commodity
- Don't hide the price behind "Select Options" — price transparency builds trust
- Don't put the price in tiny text — own it confidently

## 6. Color & Finish Swatches

Swatch selection is a critical conversion touchpoint for Sorrelli since pieces come in multiple finishes and colorways.

### UX requirements
- **Minimum swatch size: 7mm x 7mm** with 2mm spacing between swatches (Baymard)
- **Interactive swatches** — clicking a swatch should update the product image immediately. Non-interactive swatches that force a page reload add friction.
- **Show ALL available options** — 57% of top sites fail to display all swatches. Don't truncate.
- **Label clearly** — show the finish/color name on hover (desktop) or on selection (mobile). Users need to know what "BG" means ("Bright Gold-tone").
- **Indicate current selection** — clear visual state for the selected swatch (border highlight, checkmark)
- **Link to other colorways** — "Shop All [Color]" links to the full color collection

### Jewelry-specific
- Show finish AND color as separate selections (Sorrelli already does this well)
- Swatch images should show actual product material, not just a solid color dot
- Consider tooltip previews showing the product in that finish on hover

## 7. Trust Signals & Social Proof

98% of visitors to jewelry sites leave without buying. Trust is the primary barrier.

### Placement strategy
- **Star rating + review count** — above the fold, near the product name/price. This is the #1 trust signal.
- **Trust badges** — position directly beneath the product name or near the CTA, NOT buried in the footer
- **Brand coverage / press logos** — compact panel of 3-4 logos right after product identification
- **Shipping & returns** — state clearly above the fold: free shipping threshold, return window, and shipping speed
- **Social proof numbers** — "1,303 items purchased" or "47 people viewing this" — but only if authentic
- **Customer photos** — embed UGC images in the review section. Real photos outperform brand photography for trust.
- **Satisfaction guarantee** — Sorrelli's lifetime promise is a major differentiator. Make it prominent, not buried in a tab.

### Review display
- Show aggregate star rating and total count at the top
- Feature 2-3 highlighted reviews with photos in the main page flow
- Full review section below the fold with filtering (by rating, by keyword, "with photos")
- Include reviewer context when possible (e.g., "I wear these daily")

## 8. Brand Storytelling on PDP

Storytelling can increase conversion rates by up to 30%. Consumers are 55% more likely to purchase from brands with storytelling they connect with.

### Where and how to integrate
- **Product description** — don't just list specs. "From Sorrelli's Panama Rose collection" tells a story. Connect the piece to a collection narrative.
- **Below the fold, after the buy section** — this is where scrolling shoppers are building conviction. Brand story content here supports the purchase decision.
- **Value prop cards** — Sorrelli's "Hand Crafted", "Try Risk Free", "Lifetime Promise" cards are effective below-fold trust + story elements
- **Founder story** — "Woman-Founded, Woman-Led" resonates strongly with women self-shoppers. Keep it authentic and concise.
- **Craftsmanship details** — for accessible luxury, "how it's made" content justifies the price point and differentiates from mass-market

### Principles
- Story should answer "why you?" before the customer asks
- Above the fold: conversion mechanics. Below the fold: narrative and trust.
- Avoid vague taglines — be specific and authentic
- Storytelling reduces cognitive load and guides users through the decision journey

## 9. Cross-Sell & "You May Also Like"

### Effective patterns
- **"Wear It With"** — show complementary pieces (matching earrings + necklace), not random products
- **"Shop the Collection"** — link to the full color collection to encourage multi-piece purchases
- **Position after the buy section** — don't distract from the primary CTA
- **Limit to 4-6 recommendations** — more creates decision paralysis
- **Use lifestyle context** — showing pieces styled together (layered necklaces, stacked rings) prompts add-on purchases
- **"Complete the Look"** styling suggestions outperform generic "You May Also Like"

### Sorrelli-specific opportunity
The colorway system (finish + color) creates natural cross-sell groupings: "More from the Panama Rose collection" or "Also available in Bright Gold-tone"

## 10. Page Content Structure — Recommended Order

Based on the research, here's the optimal content flow for a jewelry PDP:

### Above the Fold
1. Product image gallery (dominant)
2. Product name
3. Star rating / review count
4. Price + BNPL option
5. Finish & color swatches
6. Dimensions
7. Add to Bag CTA
8. Availability / shipping indicator

### First Scroll (Conviction Zone)
9. Product description (expandable tabs: Description / Lifetime Promise / Shipping)
10. Value proposition cards (Shipping, Returns, Lifetime Promise)

### Second Scroll (Trust & Story Zone)
11. Craftsmanship / quality detail (image + text)
12. Customer reviews with photos
13. Brand story (Woman-Founded, Sisterhood)

### Third Scroll (Discovery Zone)
14. FAQ accordion
15. "Wear It With" / cross-sell recommendations
16. "You May Also Like" product slider
17. Footer

## 11. Performance

- Page load time directly impacts conversion: 1s→3s = 32% more bounces
- Compress and lazy-load images (especially below-fold)
- Defer non-critical JS (reviews widget, cross-sell slider)
- Core Web Vitals: LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, INP < 200ms
- For jewelry: high-resolution product images are non-negotiable, so optimize format (WebP/AVIF) rather than reducing quality

## 12. Jewelry-Specific Insights

- **Self-purchase framing** — women buying for themselves respond to self-reward and self-expression messaging, not gift/occasion framing. "Treat yourself" > "Perfect gift"
- **Exclusivity matters** — limited editions and small-batch messaging drives urgency. Women have a stronger desire for unique pieces over mass-produced
- **Dimensions need context** — "1.5 inches long" means nothing without scale reference. On-ear photos solve this better than measurements alone
- **Care instructions** — include but don't lead with. Expandable section, not primary content
- **Virtual try-on** — emerging pattern that reduces returns and increases conversion. Worth considering as a future feature
- **Price justification** — at the $40-150 range, shoppers are comparing against fast-fashion alternatives. Craftsmanship narrative ("hand-assembled", "crystal quality") justifies the premium
